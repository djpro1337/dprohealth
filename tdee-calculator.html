How the math works

We calculate BMR with the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, then multiply by your activity level to get TDEE (maintenance calories). Your goal sets the adjustment: a deficit to lose, a surplus to gain. Protein is set per pound of bodyweight to protect muscle, fat to a healthy floor, and carbs fill the rest.

Why GLP-1 Mode is different

On retatrutide, tirzepatide, or semaglutide, appetite suppression makes severe under-eating easy — which burns muscle alongside fat. GLP-1 Mode caps your deficit, raises your protein floor to ~1 g per pound of goal weight, and flags any target that falls below your BMR. Slower, muscle-sparing fat loss beats the scale dropping fast while you lose lean mass.
